# DeFi Research Framework

## Core questions

1. What problem is the protocol solving?
2. Who is the natural user?
3. What is the core mechanism?
4. What creates demand?
5. What creates revenue or yield?
6. What dependencies can break the system?
7. Is growth organic, incentive-led, or cyclical?
8. Does token value actually connect to protocol success?

## Analytical lenses

### Product
- Is the product clearly better, cheaper, faster, or more capital efficient?
- Is behavior repeat usage or one-time farming?
- Is the moat liquidity, brand, regulation, integrations, UX, or balance-sheet strength?

### Yield
- Source the yield precisely:
  - borrower demand
  - trading fees
  - basis / carry
  - RWAs / T-bills
  - incentives / token emissions
  - rehypothecation / recursive leverage
- Ask whether yield persists after incentives normalize.

### Liquidity quality
- sticky collateral > opportunistic TVL
- broad distribution > whale concentration
- recurring usage > campaign spikes

### Risk
Check for:
- smart contract risk
- oracle risk
- governance capture
- bridge / cross-chain risk
- liquidity mismatch
- counterparty / custodial risk
- regulatory pressure
- hidden leverage

### Token
Ask separately:
- Does the token capture fees, governance power, or only narrative?
- Is unlock pressure likely to dominate fundamentals?
- Are emissions subsidizing activity that disappears later?

## Comment-ready synthesis

Use this shape:
- Mechanism: what drives the protocol
- Edge: why people use it
- Fragility: what can break the story
- Market angle: what is underappreciated or overhyped

## Red flags

- APY discussed without source-of-yield clarity
- TVL discussed without user or revenue context
- token praised when value accrual is vague
- “institutional” used as a substitute for real demand
- growth entirely explained by points, emissions, or temporary campaigns
