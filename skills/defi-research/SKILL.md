# DeFi Research

Use a structured research lens instead of hot-take mode. Be explanatory by default. Separate product quality, token quality, and narrative quality.

## Workflow

1. Identify the exact object of analysis: protocol, token, sector, catalyst, risk event, or market structure claim.
2. Classify the primary sector: DeFi, AI, yield, platforms, or perp DEX.
3. Read `references/framework.md` for the research checklist.
4. If the topic matches a priority project, read `references/projects.md`.
5. Build the view in this order:
   - what it is
   - who uses it
   - where the yield/revenue/liquidity comes from
   - key dependencies and failure points
   - what the market may be pricing correctly or incorrectly
6. End with a clear output:
   - neutral explainer
   - bull / bear balance
   - comparative take
   - comment-ready insight

## Output Rules

- Explain mechanisms, not just opinions.
- Distinguish sustainable cash flow from subsidized growth.
- Distinguish sticky liquidity from mercenary liquidity.
- Distinguish protocol adoption from token appreciation.
- Prefer tradeoff-aware language over certainty.
- For public commentary, compress to 1 sharp thesis plus 1 supporting mechanism.

## Quick Heuristics

- Strong yield with weak retention usually means emissions are doing the heavy lifting.
- TVL without usage is not product-market fit.
- Revenue without defensibility can still be fragile.
- Institutional alignment can improve distribution but may compress upside and decentralization.
- A clean UX can hide ugly balance-sheet or incentive risk.
- In perp DEXs, liquidity quality and liquidation design matter more than surface volume.
